⚙ Policy Thresholds

Canary promotion (explore → canary)
Number of executions required before a pipeline can be promoted to canary. Prevents promoting on too little data.
Fraction of executions that must succeed (0–1). e.g. 0.95 = 95% success rate required.
Fraction of write executions whose verify_write must pass. Higher = stricter correctness gate.
Upper bound on how often a human had to correct the output. Low value = CC must be reliable on its own.
Stable promotion (canary → stable)
Executions required to reach stable. Should be higher than canary threshold — stable bypasses LLM inference entirely.
Success rate gate for stable. Typically stricter than canary (e.g. 0.97).
Verify rate gate for stable (e.g. 0.99). At stable, the flywheel bridges execution — output must be near-perfect.
Failure handling
Consecutive failures before a pipeline is flagged Critical and demoted. Triggers rollback/stop policy on write pipelines.
